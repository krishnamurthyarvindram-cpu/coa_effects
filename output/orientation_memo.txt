2. MAIN PANEL DATASET (from prior R-based analysis)
====================================================
File: merged_data/analysis_panel.rds (Git LFS — not directly accessible)
- 12,436 city-year observations
- 398 unique agencies (384 cities, some cities have multiple agencies)
- Year range: 1990–2021
- Estimation window used: 2000–2020
- 114 treated cities (COA created), 284 never-treated controls

Key variables (from prior script outputs and documentation):
  Treatment:
    - treated (binary: ever adopted COA)
    - post (binary: COA active in year t)
    - treat_year / coa_adopted_year (year of COA creation)
    - relative_time (year - treat_year)

  COA Authority:
    - invest_power (binary: can independently investigate)
    - discipline_power (binary: can independently discipline officers)
    - strong_coa = invest_power | discipline_power
    - weak_coa = treated & !invest_power & !discipline_power

  Outcomes:
    - violent_crime_pc (violent crimes per 100K)
    - violent_clearance_rate (proportion)
    - property_clearance_rate (proportion)
    - drug_arrests_pc (drug arrests per 100K)
    - discretionary_arrests_pc (discretionary arrests per 100K)
    - police_killings_pc (police killings per 100K)
    - black_share_violent_arrests (proportion)
    - black_share_drug_arrests (proportion)
    - violent_arrests_pc (violent arrests per 100K)

  Controls:
    - log_population
    - pct_black, pct_white, pct_hispanic
    - south (binary)
    - dem_power, council_pct_dem, council_pct_black
    - mayor_dem, mayor_black

============================
Current coding is binary: strong (investigate OR discipline) vs. weak (neither)
Three-tier classification available from coa_classification.csv:
  - Review-only: no investigation, no discipline
  - Investigative: can investigate, cannot discipline
  - Disciplinary: can discipline officers
